Baan IV licencing is based on concurrent users and if you have 15 concurrent licences then you can have 15 or less active baan logins at any particular time(less if there are orphan processes running in the background not freeing up licences). It is really other way round from what you said. So if 11 people are logged into baan using the same account concurrently that means you are using up 11 baan licences.

I believe the audit is more to keep you informed on the adequacy of your licences vs your usage. You need to keep track of your total licences to total users ratio, usage pattern(what is your usage at any time of the day, how many times you are getting no more licences message, if your key users are being denied logins regularly, is usage only high on a particular day of a week/month say period end etc.). In my organisation I've developed certain analytics to stay in control rather relying on external audit results.

Hope this helps.
