Dave: Well, I don't think that would be legal behaviour of Infor; of course it might depend a little on the contract, but in most of the time you buy the right to use the software, not the right to use the software *on a server* or *as long as you have support*. If anyone knows od such cases first hand, please make them public - that always helps to convince companies that inethical behaviour is not good for business.

More important however, what also depends on the contract is the right to update to a new version (in that case, ERP LN) for no additional license fees. Normally that right is in the standard support. So, lylitalo, you've got to read your contract with infor. If it's in, and if your planning to upgrade in a couple of years, you might want to stay with the contract. Infor FLEX (sort of: well-you-might-upgrade-without-having-payed-support-let's-see-what-mutual-beneficial-arrangements-we-can-find-program) probably won't be around always. Of course, if you are already planning to leave that software and use SAP (which is your own fault, then), you may as well end the support contract now.
