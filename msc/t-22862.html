The copying is quite simple.

First all demand is distributed over the working days and then the new periods are filled with the demand again.....

For example: monthly periods with 20 workable days and DF of 20 results in daily DF of 1.........(1 on a workable day and 0 on non workable days)
Copy this into a new scenario with weekly periods (with 5 workable days) and you will get weekly DF of 5

When you have different DF per month, this will result in a 'gradual' rise/drop in weekly DF in the 'breaking' week....

(at least this is how I made it 2 years ago.......don't know if someone changed it.........)
