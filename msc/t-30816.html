Hi sagartalathi,
BaanERP uses scrap & yield information to arrive at standard cost for the manufactured item. Once on production, you can record the rejected production output and this will appear in one built-in report called Production Order Rejects or something like that. BaanERP doesn't send the rejected output to its Warehousing module to be stocked. It just account the rejected output as additional production cost as compared to condition with zero reject.
Probably in short I can say that BaanERP uses the scrap & yield mostly for accounting purposes
