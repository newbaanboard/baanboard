21st March 2007, 01:03
No, you don't need to have Business Partners defined.
There are a couple of things that you must define, though.

1) in EMM, you need to define the Entity-Entity Relationship (tcemm2110m000) for the relationship-type "goods transfer" between the warehouse X in co100 from a warehouse in co200 (the warehouse the you put item A in when you complete the production order for it). Make sure you set the 'Type of order to be Generated by Enterprise Planning' to "Warehouse Transfer"--not "purchase order".

2) in co100, in EP, you need to define the Supplying Relationship (cprpd7130m000) for item A that it comes from item A in co200.

Now, as soon as you have a 'planned distribution order' for item A in co100, you should see it as 'distribution' demand in co200.

Let us know if that helps.
