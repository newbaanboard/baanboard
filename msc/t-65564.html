Jeff Ball
3rd March 2014, 12:32
Hi

MRP works a little differently from SIC. Firstly, MRP can generate recommendations for multi-level product structures, while SIC can only explode one BOM level.

MRP uses the Safety Stock field on the item rather than the Re-order Point in SIC to show the minimum stock that should be present after all demands are satisfied.

While SIC has fixed period lengths, MRP is flexible at item level by using the order interval.

Where SIC uses item level time phasing for its horizon (a multiple of the lead time plus a constant), there is an MRP Parameter to set how far into the future the MRP calculation should run. When Confirming recommendations, there is a date range setting that can limit this for individual batches.

I can't really help with documents, but I've always found the Baan IV help very useful.

Jeff Ball
11th March 2014, 18:17
It sounds as though you are using SIC forecasting. Underlying it is a normal distribution table, where the relationship of desired service level and number of mean absolute deviations of safety stock is held. The higher the desired service level, the more multiples of the MAD need to be held. When the forecast is run it calculates the safety stock needed, then adds the lead-time fill quantity to give the re-order point.

When you mention phantoms, do you mean the normal situation where the phantom is actually a subassembly that is made and consumed within the next level of manufacture without its own production order? If so, I'm not familiar with the combination of Phantoms and SIC.
