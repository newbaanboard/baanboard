Dear Forum,

We use MRP (INFOR LN 10.5) to convert demand into Purchase Requisitions. Due to the current worldwide stress in the supply chain we can no longer fully rely on the leadtimes that are agreed with our suppliers. Entering new (longer) leadtimes in de master data is not desirable so we have resorted to converting Purchase Requisitions to Purchase orders as soon as possible (so not waiting until the date the Purchase Req. should be converted).
We notice the following effect:
1. Item A has a leadtime of 10 days.
2. Demand A1 is in 30 days.
3. MRP generates a PR for Demand A1 - 10 days. So 20 days from now.
4. PR is converted to a PO today, so we give supplier 30 days leadtime (20 days extra).
5. Due to stock correction there is suddenly a shortage for Item A (Demand A2).
6. MRP generates a PR for Demand A2 but places the requirement date AFTER Demand A1 (not before even though the leadtime of 10 days could still mean the new order can be fulfilled prior to the order connected to Demand A1).

I am told that this is normal MRP behavior due to the fact/rule that MRP will not schedule orders before existing purchase orders. In order to overcome this I am now more or less told that I have to implement a full S&OP process which I am not ready for. Besides, I don't understand this MRP behavior. It goes against my logic.

My reasoning: if I want to give suppliers maximum transparency I can have a situation where I give a firm purchase order for far in the future. Supplier happy (he has an order in the future he can rely on), me happy (I can trust there is enough time to fulfill the demand on the planned time), customer happy (he gets what he orders in the time it was promised).
If another more urgent demand comes up (still within the standard leadtime of the item) I should be able to place an order earlier in the queue. I should not have to expedite the first order and replace the expedited one with a new order.

Is my reasoning wrong? Can anyone help me to understand the MRP behavior better?
