Dear Hendra,

If you have supply time of item A in its item purchase data (or specifically item supplier data) as 30 days and you simulate it in MRP with scenario start date of 15 July, then MRP will create a planned purchase order for item A on 15 July, but also said that it will arrive on 14 August. It will miss your required date of 30 July. However, all is not lost. You can set signal for it so that BaanERP will warn you when things will arrive late like this

What you have to do is go to Signal Types by Planner (cprao1110m000) and turn on the signal "Finish Date>Req Date" for the planner of item A. You can do this by choosing the planner ID as your group ID for the session and create new entry with "Finish Date>Req Date", specifying that this signal's horizon is as long as your scenario length (in days) and leaving everything else default. After doing this, whenever cases like mentioned above comes up, a warning signal will be created by BaanERP for the planner of that item, saying that a planned purchase order will be delivered late
