Speaking from experience with both IV and V, the PCS module does exactly what you are seeking. You create your standard bills of material. Then decide which of those items must be "customized" for specific orders and which can be used as standard - designating that by selecting either Anonomyous (to use as standard) or To Order (to customize) in the Item Ordering Data, Order Policy.
In PCS you create Projects, with a project equating to a specific order. For the items you have designated as To Order in the standard bill, new items are created in the customized bill. these new items are derived from the standard items and have the project number ahead of the standard item number. these customized items are treated as distinctly separate items and purchase orders and/or manufacturing orders are created specifically for them. Project costs are accumulated for the project as a whole and consist of the total of the items that are customized and those used as standard.
Have used this for manufacturing customized specialty vehicles, where 80% was standard and 20% customized and for government contracts (each separate contract was designated a separate project) where 90% is customized items.
This works well using the Project Control module under Manufacturing. Don't even consider using the Projects (often referred to as 'Big' Projects).
