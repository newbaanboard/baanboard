You can post the costs for warranty projects directly into COGS (instead of posting them to WIP first). However, you have to "throw away" the PCS/COGS (estimated or actual, whatever you're using) transactions by posting debit and credit side to the same ledger account for those projects. Just keep in mind that if you work with extra costs posted from finance to PCS (using a ledger account with the PCS integration flag set) Baan expects this account to be a balance account. As far as I know, you cannot use a profit/loss account here without using ttaad4100

I just read over your positing again: You are missing the sales part? If you don't have sales for those projects, make sure the "revenue expected" flag for those projects is set to false. Then any costs that are posted to WIP will immediately generate PCS/COGS transactions as soon as you're calculating project costs (at latest when you close the project)

Hope that helps a bit
