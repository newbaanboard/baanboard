It is better in this case to plan for the finished goods. To track the WIP, you can raise a blanket sales order and connect it to the non nettable warehouse (so that planning will not consider this demand). When actual orders are received, you can run a customized session to reduce quantity from the blanket sales order from the respective line and keep track of the remaining WIP qty.

Hi all,
thanks for providing various of useful ideas and solutions.
Eventually my team have found the best convenient solution.
solution:
1) When create item (Finished goods, with colour), inside item subentities-planning, have to tick "Dependent Demand Forecast".
2) Then the whole process can be start from special demand (WIP item: without colour item) or forecast demand not to forget normal sales order.

With this tick, every sales order will deduct from the forecast until empty or over the planning period. The sales order will be the finished item and process like other normal Sales order till the end.
