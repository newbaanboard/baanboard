seeace
7th October 2008, 11:43
Hi:
When you complete a production order, the finished material is posted against the warehouse recorded for that production order.
Shop-floor warehouse is used to receive material required to complete that production order.
In your case, (after you complete operation 2,) the finished material from operation 2 would be used in operation 3. Unless you report "order complete and post inventory to warehouse", the material does not get posted in any warehouse attached for that production order.
Shop-floor warehouses are used as "line stocking warehouses". Those are temporary warehouses which are used store material before it is used for production. "Shop-floor" warehouses are generally maintained by the department head / line supervisor and not by stores / warehouse department.
Hope this clarifies your doubts.

seeace
8th October 2008, 14:08
Generally what we call WIP is not treated as WIP in BaaN / ERPLN. In order to get the desired details, you may have to customize a report in which you have to consider:
- Input material issued to a production order and
- Output coming out of the production order.
This report would be very rough as you would have cost details for finished goods and raw / input materials only and will not have cost details for every operation.
If you want to be more precise, then you can add the operation cost per operation in this report. however this would make it more complicated and may not justify the efforts unless operation cost is very high compared to material cost.
