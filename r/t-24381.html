Thank you for this very interesting information. You will see from previous posts of mine that I am an ardent admirer and log time (10+ years) user of Baan software. From those posts you would also find that I do not have the same sort of regard for those who have managed to guide the company from its postion 10 years ago as a serious contender to the number 3 rank to the position now where the company was worth only $135mil 2 years ago and probably a similar amount today.

There is no doubt that it is a cash cow and that many other ERP's including SAP (and other SSA products) are. This doesn't mean very much other than that the customers are prepared to pay more than they may otherwise pay and that the product requires very little investment to keep it in the market.

To my mind the Baan product is not in the same league as SAP in terms of its installed base and new sales numbers or the likelihood of it still being around in say 5 or 10 years. This has nothing to do with the capability of the system or elegance of the software. It does have to do with the management of the company, its business strategies, the quality, professionalism and commitment of its people.

SSA's stated and observed strategy is to buy customer base and grow the revenue from those customers. Its strategy is not to sell product to more or new customers. Hence we see very little new name activity. I have not heard of any large scale new name customers for years. (Boeing 787 is a dwindling old name). SSA is run by a bean-counter who has a few cronies around him who evidently have very little software marketing experience. They have a business that has a very large revenue stream from annual fees that provide for future software upgrade and support services. Because the software is old, the installed base is old, the software is stable and requires almost no support. Yes it certainly is a "cash-cow" in terms of Boston Consulting's grid analysis.

On this forum most of us share a passion for the product. Especially the technical ones amongt us. We waited for years to see what Invensys would do with its large internal sale base. We were then anxious for SSA to put Baan back where it should be in the market. That has not happened and sadly it is becommig less likely as each day passes.
