Oh. I meant ttams3145m000 (Table Data Authorization).
We have implemented it to hide employee addresses from normal users (other than in the staff department).
The hidden addresses don't show on the program nor do they get printed.
The normal user doesn't even guess that there are more addresses.
So the role that's given to every user is "Not Authorized" for the table tccom130 in case the addresscode meets a certain condition.

Another role can be given to the folks of the HR department.
They have the authorization "Delete/insert/modify/read" for every addresscode ("" to "ZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZZ").

If you can limit the items to a few conditional expressions, you can use this method.
But do NOT use several conditions like


item equals 0815 and
item equals 0816 and
item equals 0817 and
item equals 0818 and
.
.
.
item equals 9999 end of expression


Than it makes no sense because you cannot foresee the length of the list in the future.
Try to have a system with the item codes e.g. to hide the items that start with "321" or "H" ( for hidden ;-) ) or all items between "500" and "699ZZZZ...". Just one or two conditions. That should be enough.

Hope it helps... ;)
