This happened to us three times last year until we finally figured out the cause. Our unix users were set up in such a way that they were the owners of the audit files when a new sequence number was created.

From time to time we would delete users who would leave our company - and deleted them using a setting that would delete all their owned files (I know, dumb huh...).

But anyway, it would corrupt the audit files and we had to clear them out to fix the auditing.
