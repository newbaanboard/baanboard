Some points to note.
1. Hardware config depends on sizing (which depends on user , modules used ,user makeup , other applications u run etc) . Though the configs look ok , it's good to get it checked by some expereinced person .

2. Since having 2 servers with SAN , would recommend u do clustering failover , which ensure good business continuity and insulates u from failures of hardware with more redundancy and little downtime.

3. In windows (even in *nix also) environs , u can some sort of loose load balancing , by actually running db server on 1 and app server on another and which actually fails over to another in case of 1 hardware failure .

4. Such failover clustering requires , that systems be in same LAN and hardware u get meets the vendors hardware compatiblity lists and 2 servers are identical without exception.
