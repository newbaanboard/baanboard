6.2 is fine :-)
If a bshell process is very busy, you may not get output in the bshcmd6.2 program.

You may check "ps -ef | grep ttaad" depending on how the jobdaemon is started...

"jobuser.1319" indicates the PID is 1319(?). You can also use bshcmd to add logging options to the bshell and see the file $BSE/tmp/bshell.1319
