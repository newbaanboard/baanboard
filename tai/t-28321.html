In most cases it doesn't affect the user processes however in certain situations I've seen some sessions crash due to a shutdown of the licence server.

Despite of this it is not recommendable to restart the licence daemon to free licences, in fact, you are using more licences than you paid for and therefore this is not legal. If Infor would find out, ( currently a lot of customers are audited by Slams.uk ) you will have an major issue.
Despite of the fact that it is technical possible to restart the licence daemon and free licences it is not legal, the only legal way is to buy additional licences or check the Baan usaga internal ( do users really work in Baan or do the same users use multiple licences ).

I actually understand your question in such a way, that you kill zombie processes and want to free the licenses bound by them. This is naturally not illegal, but quite ok.

In the recent portingsets you can use the "licmon6.1 -c" option to clear all licenses of processes, which do not exist anymore. If you have several application servers, then you need to execute this on each of them.

If you do not want to upgrade to a newer portingsets, then it is most likely enough to swap the licd6.1 and licmon6.1 binaries.
