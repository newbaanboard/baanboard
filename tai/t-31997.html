I see now. Here goes what I think is the reasoning behind it:

The dumps are always created in the VRC you are processing. Even though you are in br0p and labels affecting that form do not exist there they do exist in bra0 while the form itself is in standard making the form you need in br0p different from that in standard. The form you need in br0p is a merge of the form in standard plus the labels in bra0 and that's what Baan gives you and it puts it in br0p.

I have no clue if this works so try it carefully in some test environment:
Try running the create forms to RTDD (try with one form first) with option 2 for standard. Then run it again with option 2 for the next VRC, and so on until you reach your production level. Let me know what happens in case you decide to try.

The ugliest of options is to always create forms to RTDD with option 3 at your production VRC level. It really is very ugly because you loose track of what VRC each form belongs to at the OS level and you would now need to create forms every time you import dd dumps to a lower VRC in your VRC structure. However if you always create your forms that way you will never end up with invalid forms hiding valid ones. Again, in case it is still not obvious, I do not like that option. A place for everything and everything in its place if you know what I mean.
