You can use the input.string-command. I don't know the required portingset, but try it (we are using Baan IVc4 with the actual porting set).


Syntax:

#pragma used dll ottdllinputstr

long ret
string myvar

ret = input.string("<Header>", "<prompt>" , <size.1>, <size.2> , <ref inputvar>)

Example:
ret = input.string("Das ist ein Test", "Hallo Welt:" , 20, 20 , myvar)
The userinput will be stored in myvar.
