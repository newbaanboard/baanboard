DEM is a particularly useful tool for those users who have little understanding of Baan but just know the business processes. They can follow the models with relative ease without really knowing what's going on in the background and the steps can be laid out for them in an easy to follow format. This way they only get to see what they need as well and they don't get too much information.

It has been known that user authorisations have also been limited by not giving them access to run by session and only giving them the DEMs. This is of course not the recommended way of doing things!

The DEM process is useful. The methodology forces you to sit back and critically analyse the business, not something that you normally do in any structured way. The Business Control Model then drills down into the related processes, and so there is a unified whole.
This aspect of Baan implementation is quite impressive and compares favourably with SAP's ASAP methodology.
Having implemented, it does take a lot of maintenance, and as has already been commented upon, experienced users will not take advantage of the process flows, partly because the process viewer is slow to load.
