We have purchased the EZ-Publisher software at Herman Miller. It has been a great tool for us. We have 1200 active users and about 280 processes in DEM. With EZ-Publisher we can now distribute our business process to all employees (approx.7000) rather than just those with access to Baan.

We began our implementations 5 years ago, since then we have implemented Baan at 8 of our logistical compainies. I wish we had this tool when we first began, but I'm glad we have it now.

We are using DEM to document other business systems that we use and we can now deliver that process documentation to those non-Baan user.

Also with the business processes being web enabled we are able to use the processes in a WEB training site that we have on our corporate intranet. From the processes it is also easy to hook up task work instruction and other documnets that are releated to the process.

We are putting non-Baan processes into DEM and then exporting through EZ-Publisher to our corporate intranet. It works very well! We are using the same tools to document Baan and non-Baan apps. We also use the processes and releated activity based work instructions for our ISO 9000 documentation. Our ISO auditors liked what they saw!

Dynaflow is also in the process of releasing a product called EZ-Modeler. It has the ability to edit DEM processes without needing DEM. I've seen the tool, in the future this is the way we plan on going. For now we edit and maintain non-Baan processes in DEM and then export to our intranet site. You can also publish Visio, and some Pro-E drawings using EZ-Publisher. People can then view those diagrams without needing Visio Pro-E installed on their PC.

We have been using EZ-LIbrarian now for about 2 years. It's primary use is for managing revision control for quality documents in support of ISO certification. We also use it for vaulting and controlling dept./company policies, access request forms, service level agreements, etc. The biggest advantage is that all documents are available to employees via a web browser and searches can be performed with keywords or by functional segments. Also, all DEM business process documentation is stored in the EZ-Librarian vault and be accessed directly from the DEM diagrams as well as from the web search engine. Feel free to contact me if you have further questions.
