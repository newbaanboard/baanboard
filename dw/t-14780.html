Complementary to Ada's answer (previous reply), I wish to inform DEM users that Stand-Alone modeling of DEM models have been possible since 2001 via a tool called EZ-Modeler. Find below the main features of EZ-Modeler:

> Modeling (create & edit) of Process diagrams (BPD), Control diagrams (BCD) and Organization diagrams (BOD) is possible for any Baan IV/ERP-5/6.0 users and even non-Baan users (no Baan license required).

> In addition to DEM (Petrinet) diagrams, EZ-Modeler also enables other diagramming formats (Visio, PPT, ARIS Toolset, etc...) to be merged to DEM diagrams and to be incorporated to the Parent-Child relationships with DEM diagrams, therefore enabling a corporate-wide model covering Baan and non-Baan areas.

> Enables the import of diagrams from Baan DEM (DEM export and XML formats)

> Enables DEM native diagrams to be converted to HTML for web-based publishing of complete business models.

> Can be used in a single/centralized repository mode (GroupWare) where multiples Modelers can perform editing & sharing of diagrams, or used in a single-user mode (on your laptop on the plane).

> Enables complete employee/roles/process/activities/sessions mapping. Baan sessions layouts are even available to Modelers to facilitate the mappig of these to Process Activities (without connection to Baan).

> Enables attachement of documents/content/URL to any objects of the Petrinet digrams (BPD activities, control, states, etc / BCD functions, external agents, etc)

> Ability to perform full Diagram Revision Control with diagram lifecycle history

> Ability to perform Quantitative Analysis and Simulation on defined or imported processes.

> Already adopted by large Baan customers such as Fujitsu, Herman Miller, Solar Turbines, Conwed, etc ...


For more information or for a Trial version of EZ-Modeler, visit www.EZ-Process.com .
