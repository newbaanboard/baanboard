If you have recurring periodic payments (say for leased equipment $1000 a month for a year -Jan through Dec), how do you handle this without having to remember to enter an AP invoice for that month's payment?

Other than entering a monthly document, the only way that I can see to do it is in January, enter a document for $12,000 into AP, booking it against a prepaid account. Then set up a schedule in Maintain Payment Schedules (tfacp1103m000 or the sub-session for the document entry) for 12 payments- one a month through December). Then set up a recurring journal entry to book $1000 monthly to the lease expense from the prepaid account.

Is there an easier way to do this, or an alternative? My AP department is asking and I haven't seen anything else in Baan to handle this. We have tried setting up a PO with either 12 lines (one per month) or one line with a quantity of 12, but that seems to have just made more work since someone has to remember to receive that intangible service.

Any suggestions would be greatly appreciated. Thanks!

Hi Eddie -

I ran this by our finance consultant, Judy Miller. Here's her reply:

Find Finance/Cash Management/Supplier Payments/Processing/Maintain Standing Orders. Use Posting Method Costs. You can then set up recurring payments and these will be selected with the appropriate check run. It is a little awkward to set up because you have to do the GL Distribution for each payment but once it is set it is done.

Baan Solution number 130960 has a process flow for you.

If you have any questions on the process once in, post on up.
