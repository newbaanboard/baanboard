I think the main localisations are around protocol codes in finance, to assist in generating the legally required journal reports. You should investigate if this meets your requirements, some additional customisations may be required in that area.

We also encountered some issues where standard Baan functionality was met with resistance from local accountants;
They seemed to be very reluctant to use credit notes and they had some problems with statutory accounts (which may be addressed by the standard dual accountancy functionality in Baan V but they are using Baan IVc.

I agree with Edwin, get some local advice since not many people outside Italy understand the Italian rules and regulations.

Yes, BaaN finance is used in Italy.
The Italian localization (mainly on finance and trade issues) is certified by Ernst & Young and it is in use from 1993 (Triton 2.x) and there are many customers in Italy.
To know what are the parts implemented you can contact the BaaN Italy.
