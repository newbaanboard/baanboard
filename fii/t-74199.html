10th February 2022, 14:24
My version of what I do - there are probably some more details on the board. What I do is login via windows client, do run program and put in csh. It starts me a unix c-shell, then I just cd $BSE/bin. You should see bic_info6.2 or it might be bic_info6.1 depending how old your system is. Then I just type in the bic_info6.2 "object name" and hit return. There are lots of things you can get information on but I always just used it for libraries and session scripts. Example - the session is tisfc0101m000 and its compiled object is otisfc0101 - so I would enter bic_info6.2 otisfc0101 and hit return.

I am assuming since I just noticed you are on windows it works the same way with a cmd window.
