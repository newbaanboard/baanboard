Inventory valuation in Baan 5 is more complex than in Baan 4. There has been confusion regarding the Inventory Valuation calculation and the Cost Price calculation. The addition of Issue and Receipt Surcharges effect both calculations dependent upon the warehouses associated with the items and/or surcharges. The variances thrown when transfering stock to production orders or other warehouses complicates the understanding of these calculation differences.

There is an excellent document detailing the concepts of cost accounting, inventory evaluation, production order costing etc... I believe the code of the document is something like P3011. The document is 299 pages and has been revised multiple times. There are some minor errors in it, but overall it is very well written and 99% accurate.

We are using B4c4 with lot control, all items are valued by lotprice.
To check any discrepancies between distribution and finance inventory our accounting department monthly runs customized reports tdltc0408m000 and tdilc141m000 to check the total distribution value by warehouse and by itemgroup and compares this with the trial balance by respective dimensions.
Any disrepancies are happily dropped on my desk to find out :-)
Sorting out differences are often a nightmare, in most occassions reasons were:
- crashed BaaN sessions during maintain receipts, resulting in purchase/receipt transactions posted twice to finance
- errors in lotvalues (or LotPrice Structure by Cost price Component) in lots that were generated during the stock import we did when we went 'live', especially in written-off lots, generating incorrect lotresult postings to finance when these items were finally sold.
- errors created by bugs in a customized session to write off lotcontrolled items
So to be honest most errors were more or less our own fault, not because of BaaN standard programs :o
To ease the sorting out of any of these differences I tried to build an OLAP system using the itemcode from td stock tables and the itemcode in tfgld410, but found inconsistencies in the tfgld410.item field :mad:
