Dear adjajic,
This is easier to explain in mathematical term

Say
a=estimated qty
da=actual qty - estimated qty
b=estimated price
db=actual price-estimated price

We can easily see that
a+da=actual qty
b+db=actual price

Then
actual qty*actual price=(a+da)(b+db)
= ab + a*db +b*da + da*db

And
ab=estimated qty*estimated price
a*db=price variance
b*da=efficiency variance
da*db=calc office variance!!

Dear adjajic,

I don't know what Baan version you're in. I'm speaking in term of BaanERP 5.0c

Estimated quantity is the quantity listed in estimated material (tisfc0510m000). This is retrieved from BOM during creation of production order. These estimated material quantities will change as ordered product quantity in prod order changes. At one point in time, the estimated mat'l qty must be frozen so they can be later compared to actual qty. In BaanERP, we can choose the timing of freezing whether it'd be at creation time, at release time, or at time of first transaction.

Actual quantity is the quantity of material that's actually issued to production order. You can see quantity issued so far in Material to issue for production order ticst1501m000

Actually you can look up the comparison between est and actual qty in estimated vs actual material costs ticst0501m000. Pls remember that I'm talking in term of BaanERP 5.0c.
Also, pls bear in mind that so far, we've been talking about the material cost side only. For operation cost side, replace all the word "quantity" with "hours" in the formula

Paul
