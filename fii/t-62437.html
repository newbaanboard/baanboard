We have used the MCR extension since go live in 1999.
For the first year or so it was a nightmare as every object seemed to fail due to not being properly written for MCR. With the advent of PMC that changed and it has been pretty reliable as it will automatically download pre-requisite objects.

Functionality wise:
1. You set up 1 or 2 additional currencies to be your home currency as well as the main one - presumably USD in your case.
2. Many tables that have amounts will then have multiple versions of that
e.g. item master can have 3 values for standard cost, latest cost, selling price etc. e.g. you will have an array field tiitm001.copr(1), (2), (3)
3. When certain sessions are run, e.g. Automatic Balancing of P&L accounts tfgld6202m000 it will balance them off in all home currencies.

Hope that helps - I can give you screen shots to give you a visual idea if that would make it easier.

The purpose of the MCR extension is to have more than 1 accounting currency really... you can transact for sales & purchases without needing MCR - it will just convert the foreign currency amount on the sales invoice into your 1 home currency using the system exchange rate. The difference is that MCR will convert your sales invoice amount into more than 1 accounting currency.

e.g. Sales invoice for 1000 colones.
Exchange rate 100 : $1 US
120 : 1 Euro

In standard, your home currency is US$, so your accounting will transact a sale of $10.

In MCR, you have say US$ and Euro as your home currencies. You will see sales in the general ledger as $10 and 8.33 Euros for the same transaction.

Or Colones could be the second home currency - just trying to illustrate that you can have transactional currency and different home currencies.

The main issue as to whether you need MCR is whether you need to have accounts in more than 1 currency... Do you need to prepare local accounts in Costa Rica in local currency as well as consolidate them into the US parent is US$?
