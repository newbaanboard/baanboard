Dimensions themselves are not hard. You need to do the following:

If you have not defined a product line dimension you will need to add it (tfgld0101m000, then hit special zoom)
Set G/L accounts to require the dimension (tfgld0108m000)
In your G/L integrations there are certain choices from the list that work well for product line integrations. We have used item group for most of ours. Statistics group is also a good choice. Of course this all depends on how you are using those fields.

I would recommend that your G/L accounts are set to mandatory for these dimensions if you always want to capture it.

Doug Woeste
22nd February 2007, 21:33
I wouldn't advise it unless you are ok with having the dimension blank in some instances. If a dimension is "optional" then you will not get an integration error and the G/L and dimension will have a mixed bag of things that are fully coded and ones that are missing the dimension. From my experience the whole reason a dimension is created is for reporting purposes. If it's optional there will be things that are not categorized.
