baan is not as flexible as other ERP's when trying to use a stub year...

you can use the TFGLD0105M000 to change the year end date, but I believe you will be stuck with a 4 month long period 12...

You can also change the number of periods in the company parameters (TFGLD0101M000) from 13 to 16... you will however be stuck with 16 periods going forward. in the future believe you could just make periods 13 to 16 "correction" periods in TFGLD0105M000... I would try it out in your test environment first
