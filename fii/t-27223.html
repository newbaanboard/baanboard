Hello,
We are using all five dimensions, but very few accounts use more than 3.

Product Line
Department
Employee
Customer Group
Country


For example;
our sales and cost of sales use product line, customer group, country.
our rebates use customer group.
our expense accounts use department.
The balance sheet accounts use less dimensions than the P&L accounts.

We have written custom reports on tfgld205 to group transactions.

We have also written custom reports on tfgld106, that include the ledger and all 5 dimension values. The user then manipulates this data in Excel. By using Excel's 'Pivot Table' function, the user can group data by ledger or any combination of dimensions that they would like. This approach is really useful on expense account entries.

Hope this helps.
John

Hello,
One of the most powerfull characteristic of Baan, is the use of dimensions.
Here at our company before to use baan, the accountants use to have a "plain" chart of accounts. This mean, each account have one and just one purpose. Due to, in our bussines, the creation of costs centers is often, before baan, the acountants needs to create a separate set of account for each cost center... when we migrate to baan, the accountats have a master chart of accouts arround 10 000!

With the use of dimension, u don't need to create that. U create only one account and assigns the dimensions u need. That was a great improvement!
Nowadays we have only 2000 accounts and we use the 5 dimensions.
