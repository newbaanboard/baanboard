Hi
From your previous posts, I think your requirements are as below:
1. You get some parts from some suppliers for which invoicing and payments are made according to a Contract on monthly basis. And there is some formula involved in calculating invoice amount.
2. There are other parts supplied by the same suppliers which should have invoice as normal.

I think for the first type of items you can use Self Billed Invoice feature. You need to select the check-box 'self-billed' in Item Supplier Information session.

With this, Baan will not allow entering invoice for first type of items using tfgld1101m000. You will need to generate invoices using session 'Generate Invoices by self billing tfacp2290m000'

I assume you are on release 5 onwards,

Though this not a perfect match solution, it will meet your requirements. Do explore it further

Hi, Norman,

The way I see it, the fact that your supply manager wants an unnecessary value assigned to the nuts/bolts is the one causing all the headache. He/she can still track part visibility without any value assigned to the nuts/bolts.

Effectively, by creating journals with same dr & cr accounts upon purchase receipt and production issue, your accounting department is trying to say that they'll just treat the nuts/bolts costs as factory overhead. In my opinion, in such case, setting the unit price of these nuts/bolts to zero would be harmless to accounting. In fact, doing otherwise might result in inconsistent entries in accouting, except you do the same accounting trick (same dr & cr account) with all other possible transactions involving these nuts/bolts; including e.g. inventory adjustments
