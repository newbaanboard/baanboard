For anyone who's interested:
I am in the process of completing an implementation of EBS at a Baan IV customer, and the results look very promising. I'm starting to appreciate the work that BaaN has done previously already here, but there is much to improve in the standard logic, which is unsatisfactory to put it mildly and simply useless at times as well.
As such, the EBS-logic has been enhanced in such a way that it supports MT940 Structured as well as (an extraction of) the SEPA-specific tags included in the SWIFT MT940 Structured file as well as the useage of IBAN.
Changes have been kept as small and isolated as possible, but do require changes to the standard (without these, forget about successfully implementing EBS using MT940 in Baan).
Next step would be to improve the matching capabilities based upon the Remittance information sent in the SEPA message.
I intend to implement more intelligent logic, or use fuzzy logic to improve the success ratio of the assignment/matching process.

By the looks of it in LN, the situation is quite similar in the sense of the added value but also the limitations of EBS, and SEPA/IBAN at least is missing, and possibly also the majority of MT940 support.
