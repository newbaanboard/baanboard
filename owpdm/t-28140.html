It depends on your Bus Configuration if you enabled APS (Activation Pooling Service) or not.

If you did not enable APS then the client request will create dedicated backend process. If you do enable APS then you share the multiple requests over a single bshell connection, and it depends on the tick size etc (similar to a BW/Worktop connection and opening multiple sessions at the same time). You can enable different log/trace options on the bus components and gather statistics.

Actually we are also in a similar situation. We are developing a web-based application for our field service folks but in the first phase we will just initiate service orders in Baan but they will be able to report their hours, material and cost against it through web.
