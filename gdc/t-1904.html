This is a very interesting SOUNDING product. BUT, be very careful, database to database transactions (interfaces or migrations) are very complicated and can be very dangerous. Judging from AMB-Dataminers website I would say that you would be dealing with a VERY young company, with a very young product. This doesnt have to be bad. It is another risk to be added to the equation. The old saying  Theres no free lunch! applies in Spades when it comes to data management. For those of you that are new to the process and cannot tolerate much risk  look for companies with a proven track record.

I know I sound negative and I do wish the best for AMD-Dataminers. But, you have to remember  incorrectly migrated or interfaced data WILL shut your system down. Relational systems like Baan can not only be shut down, but damaged so bad that you could be spending years fixing the data.

FYI  There is a move in the industry to market applications like e-Cartography under the title of EAI  Enterprise Application Interfaces. There are a number of packages in this space and they all say they can do everything with not effort. They also seem to avoid the discussion of the complexities of the issue. In AMDs defense I have yet to really see any one product do such a stop the world great job. What AMD can bring to the table is a company that could really work with you and your issues.
