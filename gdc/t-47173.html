Join our free webinars to experience Migration Labs' easybaan implementation and migration methodology and framework on February 8th 2008 and February 15th 2008 at 1000h Central European Time (GMT 0900h).

More and more Baan IV and BaanERP customers are considering what to do next with their backbone ERP system. So the time has come to experience a fresh and new way to look at the possibility for migrating your Baan IV and/or BaanERP environment to ERP LN. We all know that an implementation of an ERP system from a totally different ERP vendor will be so much more expensive than a upgrade to ERP LN. With easybaan the upgrade and migration to ERP LN will be easy and cost-effective and will give the Baan users at your company the ability to learn LN so much faster than the traditional way of re-inventing that wheel again.

Benefits of the easybaan migration methodology and framework include:

At the first day of your project a pre-configured ERP LN system will be available to you containing zero-error business processes in Order Management, Enterprise Planning, Manufacturing, Warehousing, Central Invoicing and Finance and you can use your own Baan IV and/or BaanERP data right away
ERP LN training of key users will be done with your own data from Baan IV and/or BaanERP
The data migration takes place in the first phase of the project allowing customers to focus on the optimization of the implementation of ERP LN in the second and third phase of the project
The data that is migrated out of Baan IV and BaanERP can be cleaned up and optimized during the migration process. No "as-is" migration is needed
Early successes are visible at the beginning of the project
Drastically decreased implementation time and costs of ERP LN

The standard easybaan framework supports the following data that can be migrated from Baan IV and/or BaanERP:

Common and MCS tables (logistics and financial)
Product/Manufacturing related data: Items (standard & customized), Bills of Material (standard & customized), Routings, Operations, PCS Project Data
Business Partners related data: customers, suppliers and addresses
Sales Orders: open orders and history
Purchase Orders: open orders and history
Inventory Stock Positions
Open Entries from Accounts Receivable and Accounts Payable
Account balances: customers, suppliers and ledger accounts
All PCF related data
Multi-site environments (multi-logistics\multi-finance)
