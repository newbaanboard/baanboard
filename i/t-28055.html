1. When you modify source then the supplier cannot be expected to support your modifications, and only in are circumstances the original code (extent of mod will rule)
2. To modify the source you would need tools and a source code licence.
3. Read your licence carefully, modified code still belongs to the licensor, even if you corrected it or added great functionality. The IPR is with the licensor. Its dubious when not stated, but your license will (99% of the time) say it is not your code.
3a. You may also find that completely standalone code that uses other routines, includes, and accesses the Baan database (also copyright) may also be regarded as a breach of copyright.
4. Copying and supplying to any third party will be deemed to be very illegal and subject to all sorts of wrath, especially in Switerland the home of Copyright comvention!!
I would suggest that you and anyone else considering such action would need very expert legal advice, you are considering a criminal activity (maybe)
You also need a regular inventory of just what is on your system, back it up, keep change management and history of mods, who made them and when. That could just keep you out of trouble.

I suppose that sitting in India you may regard legal advice as optional, but in the real world it is advisable. The one thing you got right is that the original question was vague and thats why I suggested legal advice, from a lawyer, in Switzerland (domicile).

Copyright is covered under international law, and adapted, adopted, by most countries, including India. However each country applies its laws in different ways, some ignore them!! The question came from Switzerland, the laws there are applied far stricter than some other countries. I am in Spain, its fairly relaxed here as well!! So I would not give firm advice except see a lawyer who specialises and let him/her know exactly what you intend to do.

Now, a developer licence is somewhat different to buying source for your own use. You may indeed have a developer licence, the swiss company may not.

I can assure you that without the correct licence, and reading your contract/licence you cannot assume you have the right to modify and or write new code (which is nearly impossible to write without includes and the use of tools. It is the use of those includes that are copyright protected.

I also suggested that the Swiss company checked the licence they have signed to see if it mentions who owns any developed code, it is copmmon practice to include a section that says any modification and or new code written over the vendors database or which will not work separately to the originally supplied product(s) belongs to the vendor.

Your suggestion that in the USA the law is lenient cannot possibly be based on fact. There are many companies who are paid good money to searchthrough code to find any similarity to other peoples products, and then they SUE. Just look at recent situation with Blackberry!! A joke, but held up to the extent they nearly had to close down.

If our Swiss friend is reading this, I suggest a serious look at a tool to identify and log source code on your system, who developed it and the licence rights. Then you get a SWISS lawyer rather than an Indian amateur to look at your rights. If any potential breach is discovered simply correct it. One other thing the Indian is right on, Baan have been fairly relaxed, SSA not so relaxed and Infor --- who knows --- want to be the one to test them!!

Your point about totally new code is reasonable if you are a developer. But even then you only have the right to use source and tools on the licenced machine. You do not ahve the right to use ona client machine, which is worldwide common. You are supposed to create a development environment and then send the object to the client. There are cases where a developer has left source and tools on clients machines, the client has now inadvertently (??) broken the law by having that source on his/her machine Its no good to say I did not know it was there, its a breach of copyright.

Everyone needs to house keep, look for and manage source. Even developers!!
