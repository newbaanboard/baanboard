SSA Global Reduces 2006 Revenue Forecast
Tuesday February 21, 1:09 pm ET
SSA Global Technologies Lowers 2006 Sales Outlook, Warns 2nd-Quarter Results Will Miss Estimates


CHICAGO (AP) -- SSA Global Technologies Inc. on Tuesday lowered its fiscal 2006 revenue outlook and warned its second-quarter results would miss Wall Street expectations.
The enterprise software maker said 2006 sales for the year ended July 31, would total between $755 million and $765 million, down from its previous forecast of $780 million to $800 million. SSA blamed the lowered guidance on a strong U.S. dollar.


The company sees 2006 earnings per share of 36 cents to 40 cents. On an adjusted basis, it expects earnings per share of $1.05 to $1.09. Wall Street anticipates earnings of $1.12 per share on revenue of $793.6 million, according to Thomson Financial.

For its fiscal second quarter, SSA forecast earnings per share of 9 cents, or 25 cents on an adjusted basis, on total revenue of $188 million. Analysts are looking for higher earnings per share of 27 cents on revenue of $198 million.

SSA's stock dropped $1.57 or 9 percent to $16.20 in early afternoon trade on the Nasdaq.
