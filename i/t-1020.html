That is one question I keep getting asked these days in mails sent to me. Here is the answer:

My parents named me "Mohana Vamsi". Yes that is my first name. It is not uncommon in India to have multi-part first names. If anyone is interested "Vamsi" is the name of Lord Krishna's (a mythological figure / one of the many Hindu gods) flute. "Mohana" is melodious or mellifluous amongst other things. In the part of India where I come from, it is common to put the family name in front of the first name. And usually it is not expanded. So I used to be "P. Mohana Vamsi". When I worked for Baan I became "Potluru Mohana Vamsi" (no contractions of names allowed). Following Baan's convention of one character from the first name and seven or less characters from last name my logons were "pvamsi".

Other transformations started happening about three years ago when I set foot on the American soil. The concept of a space within a first name was alien to the American computer systems. I realised that I will be becoming "Mohana V Potluru" according to the computers. The objection I had to that was Mohana by itself is a girls name. To beat the computers I became "MohanaVamsi" and then "Mohanavamsi" (dropped the capitalization of the V). During a lean period of Baan when I was looking for projects and my resume did not even get an interview call, a friend suggested that people must be afraid to interview me because they could not pronounce my name :). So now my resume refers to me as "Vamsi Potluru".

Everyone I know calls me Vamsi. A few nicknames over the years never stuck. A mailing list "CCI - Cyber Community of India" that I was part of had a member who used to sign off with a ~Name. I did some digging to find that the tilde is used to indicate a form of the paper version of the signature. The short version of this is - my name is Vamsi. I use the ~ just as a manner of indicating my signature. The last few months I have gotten used to people referring to me as ~Vamsi.

~Vamsi
P.S.: My current client insists everyone have a middle initial. Since I don't have one, I am now Vamsi X Potluru :) - have to do anything to fit the mould.
