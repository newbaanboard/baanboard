In Singapore, we do this by a facility called Giro. Which is nothing but a standing instruction. This is what we do in Baan :

Attach the payment method to all invoices
Compose the payments

After transferring payments to tfcmg103, we create a file in the format specified by the Banker (HSBC or Citi)
This will have one debit instruction and mutiple credit instructions.
The debit instruction is to take the amount from our bank - which is equivalent to the one cheque you want to issue to the bank.

The credit instructions are for the suppliers to whom you are making the payment.
The totals of the debit and credit will be matched by the bank in their e-banking system.

In addition we can also have the payment details by supplier embedded into the file so that the supplier knows which invoices are being paid.

Of course, all these are at a cost charged by the bank per transaction.

A somewhat equivalent facility in India is the ECS instruction.

Hope this helps.
