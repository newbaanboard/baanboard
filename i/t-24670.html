Basicall SIM 1, 2 3 are three major presentations during the project, where you demonstrate the processes in Baan. Roughly they are scheduled at 1/3 (SIM 1) of the implementation time, 2/3 of the time (SIM 2) and immediately before going life.

Roughly spoken, you can do the simulation phases in this way:

Simulation 1) The major process (from sales order entry to sales invoice integration into finance) for a single, but typical product of the company is demonstrated by the implementation partner, the basic layout of the way working with Baan is clear

Simulation 2) The refined process is shown by the key users, the details of working with Baan are clear

Simulation 3) The end users are trained, The process is demonstrated by the end users all doing their part of work within the process. All issues are cleared. If everything looks fine then, managements gives the ok for going life.
