Vish

I think it's functionality. Service component movement does not create stock transactions; it's a very simplified way of recording which serially numbered assemblies are mounted on which installations. Say you have a machine shop with three lathes sharing ten drill heads, you can just about record which drill is on which lathe, and of the ones not mounted, which are ready for use and which are in refurbishment. It's a way of extending the knowledge of the structure of the maintained unit beyond just locations and installations. From 5.0 onwards the whole concept is scrapped and replaced by a 99 level configuration structure where you do have material movements between maintained assets and the inventory environment.
