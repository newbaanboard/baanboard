Jan Baan owns Cordys (formerly Triffit) and these guys have created a new Web Services platform called Cordys WCP.
This product does everything OpenWorldX will do in two years from now. They also employ most of the ex-Baan people who designed and created the OpenWorld platform many years ago.
So, SSA is not stupid and it will be easy to replace OpenWorldX with WCP since they are already doing stuff OWX promises to do in the future.

Recently Cordys also started to develop new applications in WCP as well. These will be add-ons (Automotive, A&D, Service & Maintenance, Lean Manufacturing, etc.) that are basically platform independent and will allow many existing Baan IV customers to move to Baan V or higher and still use an industry specific application on top of their Baan installation. Today in Baan's standard offering for Baan V and Gemini they do not even address these verticals.
Another reason for Jan Baan to talk to SSA.

The existing Baan customer base is for 80% on Baan IV so with Cordys WCP almost everybody can be satisfied.
Those who want to leverage their existing IT investment use Cordys WCP to link other applications to Baan IV and there WCP serves as the Web Services product.
Those who are on Baan IV with an extension like Automotive can now go to a higher version of Baan and use these Cordys add-on apps for their industry specific needs.

Like with .NET the WCP platform is not only a Web Services platform it also becomes a programming language.
Very powerfull!!!

IMHO it is a matter of money that dictates the stay on BaanIV, as much as the stability of the software. Just think about the IT expenses of the last couple of years. (Here in Europe!)

First we had to buy B4, and implement it. Also, since there are definitely some underdeveloped areas in B4, customizations had to be built, and maintained. Then, after B4 came the Y2K glitch. Again a considerable amount went to IT. In the meantime, we had to keep updating B4 with the latest patches, and also modify our custom stuff to go along with it. Then came the Euro conversion. With the state of the first conversion software that Baan released, it cost quite a lot of money to try to get it working. The later updates performed better, but still we had to test and retest... And all the time the patches kept coming...

So now, when we have a reasonable stable platform, we need some time (years) to get at least some ROI from IT! And without spending thousands more to install a new package like Gemini. I call it "new", and that is what it is. Baan can say a thousand times that there is a seamless upgrade path from B4 to Gemini, but I don't believe them. Since the software is changed so drastically, there are huge areas that will have to be optimized and basically re-implemented to benefit from the new stuff. A conversion is never enough!

As for upgrading all customers before 2008, forget it! There are still customers on Triton 2.2, who are not even considering a move to B4 or B5, so forget about Gemini. And don't forget that ManManX is basically T2.2 as well, so all SSA's customers are in fact running T2.2! As for the AS400 mentioned earlier in the thread, again, forget it! The small and midmarket doesn't have the money to invest in completely new technology!
