Sounds right to me.

Baan, for a while, delivered some source code with patches. but they never delivered the include files that would be required to compile that code.

If you managed to get the include files and then compile the code you'd be out of compliance.


There is another option that you may want to propose to them. They have a customization wing of their consulting. You could pay them to create "custom sessions" for you based on your sessions, that would be legal and allow you to to basically continue un-interrupted.

The upside to this is

1. You don't have to purchase the source for the entire app (it was $1 million back in the day).

2. You could work it so that you got support for the customized software (i.e., they'd upgrade it when you wanted to apply patches)

3. You don't have to rollback your customizations.

4. It should be cheaper than the other options since it's usually $$ for time and they just need to take your code compile it and package it for you.

If Infor has left a bad taste in your mouth after the audit, which is understandable, you could go to a 3rd party customization partner and work out what I've mentioned above.

Source code modification without it's purchase or proper authorization is a violation of eula whether the sources were delivered through patches or consultants left .

However if u closely check ur customizations , u can do the same through AFS too . In AFS u can virtually do almost everything (barring few limitations). But that certainly entails writing ur code to use AFS to call function servers of the sessions u customized . This alternative certainly requires some re-coding.

Writing ur own code does not in any violate EULA as long as u do not take any single source developed by vendor.
