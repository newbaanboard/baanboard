One thing that concerns me is that your output shows spaces before and after the number (in addition to the commas). Are you doing some sort of concatenation that is adding the commas and, perhaps, the spaces? Another concern is that the str$ usually doesn't add superfluous spacing. Hmmm...

As for the number and the spaces, you should shift before you strip. The strip$ function strips white spaces from the right side. So, if you shiftl$ first and the strip$ after, that should get rid of the spaces.
