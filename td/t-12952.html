BA - ASCII user, not applicable in your situation, to connect to Baan with a telnet session
BX - (X)Windows based user. You should have 20 of those.

With ttiex3100, you specify whether Tools Development is activated or not. During patching this switches the development sessions on or off.

Hi.

My opinion is also that you should contact Baan.
I work mainly with NT/W2K environments and if someone buys for example 10 licences they give him a validation key with 10 BX licences, not 9.

The development licence is a different thing. It is a switch to activate some development sessions. If someone is doing development, it uses one of the BX licences.
For example, you cannot have at the same time, 10 "normal" users plus 1 development user if your licence has only 10 BX.
