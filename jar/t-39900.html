The IT department of Thermo Fisher Scientific in Asheville, NC has an opening for an energetic Manufacturing & Distribution Business Analyst. He or she will join a team IT professionals that together supports a growing and exciting life-sciences business that includes several manufacturing plants and a North American divisional sales and service organization.
The Business Analyst functions as a liaison between the business and IT. This role requires in-depth knowledge of the planning, purchasing, manufacturing and logistics business functions and the systems that enable their processes. The Business Systems Analyst is a change agent who utilizes his or her knowledge of business and IT to drive process improvements leveraging best-practices and technology to their full extent. As the IT owner of the manufacturing and distribution applications, the Business Analyst also provides day-to-day application support, troubleshoots problems and works with software vendors and programmers to provide solutions. The ideal candidate has a minimum of 5 years experience as a business/systems analyst, consultant or equivalent in the areas of supply chain management, planning, manufacturing, procurement, quality and/or logistics. This position requires extensive experience with ERP systems (Baan). The ideal candidate is an energetic self starter with strong principles who wants to work in a dynamic but challenging team environment. Business Systems Analysts report to the Manager of IT. Relocation assistance is available for eligible candidates within the US or Canada.

Summary of Functional Responsibilities:

 Drive best practices, process improvements and system optimizations in the areas of sales, planning, manufacturing, procurement, logistics and across the supply chain.
 Analyze, model and document business processes using on-line business process management tools.
 Analyze, design, select and implement system enhancements, bolt-on applications and/or commercial software packages to enable and optimize business processes.
 Provide leadership and guidance in defining and implementing data standardization rules and enforcement mechanisms.
 Design and implement metrics, cubes & reports to provide management with accurate and timely steering information to manage the business.
 Develop SQL queries for ad-hoc reporting and data analysis.
 Act as project manager for IT intensive business improvement projects.
 Provide periodic class room training on supported systems.
 Provide day-to-day process and application support to key users within the sales, planning, procurement, manufacturing, and logistics departments.
 Troubleshoot technical problems with applications and data and interact with software suppliers and programmers to resolve these problems.

Experience and Education:

 A minimum of 5 years hands-on experience as a business/systems analyst, consultant or equivalent in the areas of supply chain management, planning, manufacturing, procurement, quality and/or logistics. A bachelors degree in computer science, management of information systems, business, management science, operations management, engineering or equivalent work experience. APICS certification is a plus.
