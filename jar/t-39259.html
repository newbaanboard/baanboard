The IT department of Thermo Fisher Scientific in Asheville, NC has an opening for an energetic System Administrator. He or she will join a team IT professionals that together supports a growing and exciting life-sciences business that includes several manufacturing plants and a North American divisional sales and service organization. The system administrators primary responsibility is the day-to-day operational management of mission-critical business/ERP systems (Baan) and ensuring their availability, security, performance and operation. In addition he or she will be involved in IT processes documentation and Sarbanes-Oxley audit and compliance activities. On the job system specific training will be provided. This is a full-time permanent position and it has the possibility to grow into a senior position. The system adminstator reports to the manager of IT. Relocation assitance within the US or Canada is available for eligible candidates. For for information on Thermo Fisher Scientific, please check www.thermofisher.com.

Functional Responsibilities:

 Monitor the proper functioning, performance, security and back-ups of mission-critical business systems.
 Maintain user accounts and access authorizations to operation systems, databases and business applications.
 Install software patches and customizations per defined change management procedures.
 Write and update IT system administration procedures.
 Define, maintain and monitor batch jobs and job schedules.
 Monitor and maintain printer and device setups and manage print queues.
 Communicate with the organization about major system events.
 Enforce Sarbanes-Oxley compliance rules, collect compliance data and execute periodic compliance processes.
 Assist in troubleshooting system problems.
 Work with software and outsourcing companies to resolve system issues
 Participate in process and system improvement projects

Essential Functions of the Position:

 Ability to sometimes work on weekends and holidays, as needed.

 Bachelors degree in a computer related field. Work experience and/or technical certifications could be substituted for a degree. Minimum of 3 years hands-on experience.
