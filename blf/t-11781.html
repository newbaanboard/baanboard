recalculate prices and discounts tdsls4810s000
SITUATION DESCRIPTION:

How does the session tdsls4810s000 work?SOLUTION DESCRIPTION:
When a sales orderline is created Baan searches for prices/ discounts in the following order:
1. Special contract (tdsls3102s000)
2. Normal contract (tdsls3102s000)
3. Maintain prices by customer and item (tdsls0101m000)
4. Maintain discounts by customer and price group (tdsls0102m000)
5. Maintain discounts by customer (tdsls0103m000)
6. Maintain prices by pricelist and item (tdsls0104m000)
7. Discounts by pricelist and pricegroup (tdsls0105m000)
8. Discounts by pricelist (tdsls0106m000)
9. Prices by item (tdsls0113m000)
10. Sales price in item data (tiitm0101m000)

As soon as Baan finds a price it inserts this price and starts searching for the discount. Again when Baan finds a discount it stops searching. Please note that when a discount of 0% gross has been defined then Baan will continue searching for a discount. If a discount of 0% nett is defined then Baan will stop searching.
An example: a sales price is only defined in item data (tiitm0101m000). Discounts (larger than 0%) have been defined in session maintain prices by customer and item (tdsls0101m000) and in maintain discounts by customer and pricegroup (tdsls0102m000). Baan will search from point 1 till 10 and will find a sales price in item data and will display this price as default sales price in the sales orderline. Baan will then find a discount in point 3 prices by customer and item. This discount will also be displayed as default in the sales orderline. If you run the session Recalculate prices and discounts tdsls4810s000 when all sales orderlines have been inserted the system looks in a different way. Please note that in below mentioned examples only 1 discount level has been defined. It is possible in Baan IV to define 3 discount levels.
First of all the system will not search on line item basis. It will first accumulate all quantities and amounts for the same items or pricegroups. Then it will start searching for the "highest" structure where it can find a discount for these totals.
For example:
Customer 1010
Item X
Pricegroup SLS
Sales price in maintain item data: 100 Euro
The following price/ discounts have been defined:
Prices by customer/item tdsls0101m000:

Quantity 9999999 price 95 Euro discount 20% parameter set to "line item"Discounts by customer and pricegroup tdsls0102m000:

Quantity 9999999 discount 30% parameter set to "order"Discounts by customer tdsls0103m000:

Quantity 999999 discount 15% parameter set to "order"A sales orderline is created for 10 pieces of item X Baan will find a price and discount in the session prices by customer/item first and will enter a default price of 95 Euro and a default discount of 20%.
However when you run the session recalculate prices and discounts Baan will not stop with the first discount it finds but it will search for the "highest" discount it can find.

In this example when you run recalculate prices and discounts Baan will find a discount in the session discounts by customer. Therefore Baan will change the discount percentage to 15%. The discount Baan found when the sales orderline was created will not be taken into account now.
Please note that you can define 3 parameter settings when you fill the sessions mentioned in point 3 till 9: by line, by order or both (line and order). When you run the session recalculate prices and discounts Baan will only take into account the parameter settings to order or both. When you only have 1 discount structure defined for example prices by customer and item you can use the session recalculate prices and discounts for example in case you sell items which have a different delivery schedule or have the same pricegroup. In this case you set the parameter to order or both.
For example you have the following discount structure for item Y and customer 1010:
Prices by customer and item tdsls0101m000 parameter set to order:
Quantity up till 10 price 100 Euro discount 5%
Quantity up till 30 price 100 Euro discount 10%
Quantity up till 50 price 95 Euro discount 15%
Quantity up till 100 price 90 Euro discount 20%

Quantity 9999999 price 80 Euro discount 25%
60 pieces of item Y are sold with 3 different delivery dates. Therefore you have to insert 3 sales lines namely 1 sales orderline for 10 pieces, 1 sales orderline for 20 pieces and 1 sales orderline for 30 pieces. Should you have the parameter set to line then a price of 100 Euro will be calculated and a discount of 5% for the first orderline and a discount of 10% for the following 2 orderlines. However when you have the parameter set to order/ or both and you run the recalculate prices and discounts session a price of 90 Euro and a discount of 20% will be calculated.

The Answer to your Example was:This is standard functionality.
When determining the discount for an orderline, systems searches from the most detailed level to a more general level; the fisrt discount found is taken. In this case, a discount by Customer and Price Group is found.
When recalculating, system looks again from a detailed to a general level, but now stops at the most general level, which is applicable for all lines.
In this case, this is the Discount by Price List! (on level 1 and 2)
So, nothing wrong here.
