What is the purpose of LIFO/FIFO method of inventory valuation in BaaN V and how does it work?

Flip_J
24th July 2002, 10:04
It is an attempt to introduce actual costing in Baan V.
Say you bought an item once for 10 and then for 20 some time later. You now sell the item, the cost of the item sold will be 10 if FIFO (First in first out) or 20 if LIFO (Last in first out).

Antoon
24th July 2002, 10:58
Yes it does. If you use FIFO the stock will be valued against the purchaseprice and surcharges of the items purchased most recently.
If you use LIFO then Baan will check what the data are of the oldest stock available and value against these prices and surcharges. This has nothing to do with the actual items in stock, these may be old or young. It only calculates what are the oldest items administratively in stock since the last time the item was out of stock of course.

EG - FIFO
Receipt 1: 100 at 5 ea
Receipt 2: 100 at 6 ea
Inv value 100x5 + 100x6 = 1100
Issue1: 150 (100x5 + 50x6 = 800)
Remaining value: 50x6 = 300

EG - LIFO
Receipt 1: 100 at 5 ea
Receipt 2: 100 at 5 ea
Inv Value: 100x5 + 100x6 = 1100
Issue 1: 150 (100x6 + 50x5 = 850)
Remaining value = 50x5 = 250
