In the "Sales Control (SLS)" module, invoicing by installment allows you
to create invoices for amounts - beforehand or afterwards - without the
concomitant physical delivery of goods.
Whether or not the customer is to be invoiced by installment can be
indicated in the sales order header. If invoicing by installment is
desirable, you can add a number of installment lines to the order. An
installment line consists of an amount and a number of additional
details. Such lines can be placed on an invoice whenever this is
desired. Billed installments are settled (subtracted from the goods
amount) when the goods are delivered and invoiced. Settlement can
take place in two ways.

Indirect Settlement
In this case, delivered goods are not invoiced until the installment
order receives the status "Closed". However, the customer
can be invoiced for one or more installments in advance. When the order
receives the status "Closed" the invoiced installments are
subtracted from the goods amount on the invoice. The advantage of this
method is that deliveries of goods need not be immediately accounted for
by billed turnover. A disadvantage is that the revenues from the sales
of goods only become visible in the package "BAAN Finance" the moment
the installment order is closed. Graphically, this could be represented
as follows:

| installment order is closed |
| customer invoiced for goods and |
| installments |
| (guarantee) installments are settled |
+----------------------------------------+
| | |
| V |
-+--------------------------------------------------------------------+
|<- customer invoiced for ->|<- guarantee installments are ->|
| installments, not for | invoiced |
| goods | ---------> time |


Direct Settlement
Billed installments are directly subtracted from goods deliveries. If
the installment value that has been billed is less than the value of the
delivery, the customer will receive an additional invoice for this
amount. In this method, the revenue from sold goods is immediately
apparent within the "BAAN Finance" package. A disadvantage is, that
each delivery must be directly accounted for by billed revenue.
Graphically, this could be represented as follows:

| Installment order is closed |
| Customer invoiced for goods and |
| installments |
| (Guarantee) installments are settled |
+----------------------------------------+
| | |
| V |
-+--------------------------------------------------------------------+-
|<- customer invoiced for ->|<- guarantee installments are ->|
| goods and installments; | invoiced |
| installments settled directly| |
---------> time


Two types of installment lines are possible: normal installments and
guarantee installments. The former are billed before or simultaneous
with the goods. Guarantee installments can only be billed after all
normal installments and goods have been delivered.

After delivery of the physical goods and after the customer has been
invoiced for the normal installments, the installment order must be
assigned the status "Closed". This can be done in the
session "Maintain Installment Data (tdsls4106s000)" by choosing option
"Continue". Changing the status has various purposes:


- If the amount for which the customer has been invoiced is too high,
a reverse entry will be created, and a correction installment will
appear on the invoice
- Guarantee installments for which the customer has not yet been
invoiced are subtracted from the invoiceable amount
- Invoices can be created for guarantee installments

For details regarding the tax consequence for tax provider users, see
subfunction Tax Impact on Sales Installments for Tax Provider
