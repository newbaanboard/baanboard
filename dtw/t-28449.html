We, like many Baan users have found that the standard Baan reports do not always include what we want in the formats we want, so we mostly use reports we have written ourselves, using a separate report writer  we have used both crystal and cyber query with great success. But  if you must have a Baan report:

GO TO tdsls5501m000  Created Sales Order History by B P and Item
Menu Path
General Menu
Order Management
Sales Control
Sales Order History
Session  Created Sales Order History by BP and Item
When in the session go to the menu bar and select:
File:
Print:
Print Sales Order Line History by Sold-to BP and Item

This brings up a dialog box where you can enter your desired ranges (or single value ranges) for BP; Date Range; Item or Item Range or any of the other selection options.

All excellent suggestions about using standard baan reports and possible customizations.

But - in my humble opinion I would avoid customization of the Baan reports and the consequent problems with all customizations - are they going to continue to function as desired with the implementation of the next service pack?

My experience says that you are much better off investing in a good report writer (have used both Crystal and Cyber Query with great success). You can train many users to do some of their own ad hoc report writing and can get the specific information and formatting as you want much easier than via customization of the Baan report scripts.
