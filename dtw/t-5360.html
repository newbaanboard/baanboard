When you enter the sales order you can zoom of the sales order line and select the option to generate planned purchase orders. It is also possible to use the session generate planned purchase orders for sales orders to generate a purchase order of the whole sales order. Then confirm and transfer the planned inv purchase order. In the maintain purchase order line session on one of the forms you should then see the associated sales order and line.

Hello PJ,

As I understand you would like to use the MRP and SIC planning methods in Baan to optimise your inventory and to secure the SO. I could suggest several things which will let you be more flexible. Of course, it will be up to you to decide how applicable they can be in your case, so far as I am not familiar with it in detail.
1. Since your PO are linked so tightly to SO why don't you use the PCS module? Then everything will be project oriented.
2. How applicable it is in your case to use the MRP sales forecast (or even MPS)? Can you receive from your customers any sales shedule, which could be rough and made on a monthly or weekly basis but will provide you with an idea what are going to be your requirements to your suppliers, especially for items with long lead time periods or in cases when large amounts are ordered.
3. Also you could use all the available order parameters like time fence planning, higher re-order points, safety time cushions and so on to make your deliveries less "painfull" and unpredictable.
4. Even DRP is a solution depending on the case.

All these are just ideas but you may find something usefull for your case.
