My memory is not very clear after 10 years of Manman/x and Baan. But I do remember that PRMS had the facility of having at least one Purchase Burden (overhead) that depended on other factors.

The operation where I used this charged various overhead percentages to its "branches" for products that were purchased centrally. This additional cost was related to factors such as Item/Product group codes.

I think PRMS was very flexible in regards these cost elements which were very much like baan's cost componnets. For example I think it was possible to have numerous overhead components on manufactured items rather than the 2 that BaanIV has.

Sorry my memory is not more lucid but it may be worthwhile checking with SSA to get a better idea.
