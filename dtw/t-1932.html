Updating your standard cost is a Business decisiion that effects several areas within your system and company.

Updating the costs will revalue your inventory, effect your PPV's and could effect sales prices and margins. (depending on the setup)

Generally speaking cost should be updated yearly in a manufacturing environment but some environments where material cost change rapidly and to great degrees may need to look at a semi-anual or quarterly update.

Hi -

We revalue our standard costs for all items at the same frequency that we adjust our overhead rates. Historically, this has been once per year. However, if we have a purchased item whose cost had a significant change, we will revalue as needed so that our inventory more accurately reflects it's true value.

As the others have mentioned, your country's tax laws may play a significant roll in the frequency that you will need to recalculate your standards.

Hitesh Shah
9th September 2003, 12:25
When Baan revalues items , Baan does following
1. Calculates new cost price
2. For the existing inventory of the item in question it will generate warehouse/revaluation in integration transaction for the difference with new cost price.
3.Update production WIP with new cost price and generate production / revaluation integration
4. Update standard cost prices history
5. Update cost prices in item master table

If your inventory figures by warehouse are wrong, then warehouse / revaluation may be wrong.

When u purchase / sell items with back dated reciept / delivery then system generates Purchase/revaluation and sales revaluation respectively in integration (when the standard cost price has been revised after the back dated receipt/delivery date).Same about PRP warehouse / revaluation and Replenishment / revaluation back dated transactions.

Please note this applies only to standard item (not customized items) inventory.
