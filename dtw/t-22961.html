25th January 2005, 17:26
Be very careful in using the MRP sales forecast. It is not consumed by real sales orders as it is in MPS. For example: If you forecast 100 units in a specific period and you get orders for 100 units in the same period you will get planned MRP orders for 200 units in that period. You can use this forecast as a future planning tool if the likelyhood of getting real orders within the forecast period is low. Note the field in MRP that allows you to determine the start date of the forecast.
