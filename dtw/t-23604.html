Hi Riaz,

MAUC will be updated every time there's inbound transaction. If it's purchase receipt, then MAUC will be updated with purchase price. Otherwise, standard price will be used (which itself can be average, last purchase price, current purchase price, or simulated price; depending on how you set it)
Hence, correcting MAUC is indeed hard and you'll find that sometimes you have to resort to using dummy PO to achieve this. It's not really that neat, but we have limited choice. The high data fidelity that ERP systems like BaanERP require can be good, but also tedious to correct, I guess

Rgds,
Paul
