I suggest you inventory the item in weight rather than rolls or any other unit. That way you would be able to receive the actual quantity.

LN does not allow you to define a storage unit the way it was in earlier versions, so the alternative to this is to use lot control if you need to know how many units are in stock (how many lots)

Hi,
Paul is right.
For more information, at one of our clients place, CRCA Rolls are used for manufacturing.
So the Purchasing, Inventory & Issue to production is done in base unit of "kgs" only. Normally the supply comes in the form of ROLL only, but transactions are done in Kgs only.
Generally in these industries, what matters most is Rate in Rs/ 1 Kg of CRCA, hence its only feasible to control it that way. The weight variation per ROLL dont matter much, as we pay against the actual weight. Also for added working, the Suppliers are instructed ( out of Baan ) to Supply the material in size ( outer dia/ inner dia/ reqd width ) & the weight ( 250 Kgs minimum ). Its working fine.
