In Baan IV, there are 3 major tables from which Baan takes the value (when you look at the session tdilc1512m000).

These are tiitm001 (total item inv)
tdinv001( inv by warehouse)
tdilc101 (inv by location and warehouse)

If there are few items, these tables can be changed according to tdinv700.

Sorry to take so long to get back to you. It has been a while since I've seen the report (you don't see it unless there are errors).

As I recall, it assumes that the inventory in warehouse inventory by item (tdinv001) is correct, and adjusts the inventory level in the item master (tiitm001) to match it. In our case we don't sue lots, locations or containers.

You can verify this yourself by comparing the report to a session like tdinv0510m000 "Display Warehouse Invnetory by Item", or the equivalent session for your inventory setup. If you look at this session for the items on this report, you will see that the total line (which comes form the item master) does not equal the sum of the detail lines. This should give you an idea where the old and new values are coming from.

Hope that helps.

1. Item master shows the inventory at the company level. Thus if you have more than one warehouses for similar items then the inventory figure of one warehuse may not tally with the inventory as per item master.

2. You have stated that you you are not using lot control but have you implemented location control?

3. The session tdinv0252m000 is in 3 parts. The first part shows the difference between item master inventory and inventory as per tdilc101
The second part shows the difference between inventory as per tdilc101 and tdinv001 by each warehouse. The third part is not applicable to you as it shows difference between lot inventory

4. Old inventory for the first part means the existing inventory as per item master and for the second part means inventory per tdinv001

The best way to rectify this is through a cycle count. You may want to experiment with this with inventory adjustment for a single item
