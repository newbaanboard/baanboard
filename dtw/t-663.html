For the orno.t field, try:

(1) Set the default to 0 (this prevents the wide open range)
(2) Set the Inp/Ref expression field (under default) to 0. This prevents entry in the field. The value will change automatically with entry in orno.f

Unfortunately, this would limit your ability with other types of orders. If you wanted to get specific with production orders, you could change the input/ref field to not (koor.f=tdilc.koob.act.sfc and koor.t=tdilc.koob.act.sfc). Then, if your range was production order to production order for order type, it would prevent entry in the orno.t field.

Just an FYI and a friendly suggestion. I would suggest any session that is a process function that you put a or a couple of key defaults that force the users to put in exactly what they want, even if they want a range. We have done 3 implementations (Triton, 4b, 5b and soon 5c) and this is one of the first things we do. There is nothing worse than getting the phone call from someone new to the system or having just having a bad day tell you they just lit up a global order transfer or as stated above outbound, or checkrun by accident. We have tried our best to dummy proof the system, since at times in testing I have been the dummy lighting up one of these types of runs. Being a user I always found it annoying that Baan expected everyone to be perfect. Trying to reverse things if someone makes a mistake in this system is very difficult if not impossible. On top of that they choose a default in print and process of wide open. One would figure that with as many trouble calls as they have to solve that they might understand that that the world is not perfect and they we as users need a way to deal with mistakes other than having to go into general table maintenance to undo things. Until then we will have to do our best to remove as many opportunities for error as possible by putting in the 0-0 defaults.

Release Outbound is one session where accidents are very common. I did face several horrible situations at customer locations as I work as a consultant. You may try the following:

- Block access rights to the main session 'Release Outbound' to all users and allow only the subsession 'Release Outbound' access rights to specific users who need to issue materials.

- Use order no series specific to department and user(to the lowest level possible). Allow access rights for the applicable order series and order type only for table data.

i hope this helps.
