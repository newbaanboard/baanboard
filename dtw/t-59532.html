Thanks for your reply. The reason why we don't want to include the rejected inventory in the inventory on hand is because it's still factored in the economic stock. Please refer to the example below:

Inventory of Item AAA
On Hand = 100 pcs
Blocked = 10 pcs
Allocated = 50 pcs
Economic Stock = 50 pcs

If you see this, you might think that you still have an available inventory of 50 that you can allocate to other order. But the truth is you only have 40 left because 10 of it was rejected. The economic stock should be 40 not 50.

soujanya_p
18th November 2010, 11:46
Hi Reeyah,

I meant the same in my previous update. If you want to maintain rejected inventory it will always be included in inventory on hand and will reflect in economic stock.

The only option to remove it from inventory on hand is to remove the parameter. But in that case you will not have the history of rejected inventory transactions whether they were returned / reaccepted / destroyed.
