The safety stock is the stock that should never be touched. The reorder point ("Meldebestand") is the stock level at which you will have to order. When you order at this point, the new delivery will appear exactly when your safety stock is reached (in case theres an "optimal" stable demand).
So the safety stock is just used for calculating the reorder point, which is actually the stock level that SIC reacts on.

Hello,

Reorderpoint and safetystock don't have a relationship in regards to the reordering. reordering is done according to the planned delivery and the leadtime for ordering new stock (or manufacturing). The moment in time where the stock is below the reorderpoint, new stock will be issued (incl. leadtime).
In my opinion safety stock is used in MRP/DRP.
