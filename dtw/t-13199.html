I have used Baan Quotations for exactly this reason.

You enter the quote (looks just like a sales order).
By setting a success percentage on the quote, you can determine if MPS / MRP will see this demand. So, you can run MPS/MRP with a minumum success percentage of 99 % and only see 'sure bets' and then run it with a success percentage of 1 % to see everything.

Once the credit hold issue is resolved, then you can process the quote into an order.

The problem with this approach is that it does require some hand-holding / manual intervention to watch the credit of the customer, and then process the quote into an order.

An uglier work around involves changing the warehouse on the sales order to be a 'non-nettable' warehouse, and then once the credit issue has been resolved, change it back to a nettable warehouse.
