Hello,
In my opinion, Oracle beats SQL in all fields. Oracle is the Rolls Royce of the RDBMS. And quite expensive!

Obviously, it is not the RDBMS for all (Not matter what Oracle says). All depends on your infrastructure and your applications.

In small-middle environments, sql is a good solution. Small administration, acceptable performance and high compatibility with others ms products.

But in middle-big-huge environments Oracle is better, much better I dare to say. In this kind of environments, Unix (any flavor) and Oracle, gives you an affordable administration and performance.

I want to share my experience on this.

For Baan, I have tasted, Informix and Oracle, due to I never have used windows for my ERP. Now I have been installed SL8 (another Infor ERP) with SQL in a two Dell boxes with 2 Intel six core processors with 16 gb of ram each one. Both of them are using MSWINDOWS 2008 enterprise server. For the database we are using MS-SQLSERVER 2008.

The result: during the implementation, several crashes and poor performance between application-db server.

Finally, the implementation environment (SL8, MS-WINDOWS, SQL2008) have not the same performance against my production environment (BAAN, REDHAT, ORACLE). I have to add, my Linux boxes have just 2 intel quad core processors.

Hope this helps.
