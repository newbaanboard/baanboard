Unless you have a specific need, I'd recommend going with what most people are using because that gets the highest level of testing and attention/support from the vendors.

For NT seems like that would be SQL Server.
For Unix (AIX, HP, Linux) seems like that would be Oracle.

I say seems based on my own past experience and postings as to what combinations most ERP-LN customers seem to have. So although Informix is as good an RDBMS as Oracle (won't debate that here), there don't seem to be as many people running ERP-LN on Informix as on Oracle. Again, I don't have hard numbers hence the word seem. You could talk to your Infor rep and perhaps they could give you numbers or post a survey here or attend an Infor conference and ask around.

See the notes regarding the latest porting set for ERP-LN and the RBDMS and Operating System notes along with any Infor Roadmap documentation (as well as general IT trends) and they will give you a better idea of support now and in the future.

Again, try to avoid having a unique combination (hardware, OS, RDBMS, ERP) as that causes support issues. For example in the 90's, I knew of a company that had 3 groups make selections separately whereby they picked from their sole perspectives the best hardware & OS, the best RDBMS, the best ERP and when it all came together (with no final, summary review) they had something totally unique (Sequent, dynix/ptx, Informix (using level 1 driver), Baan IV) that apparently no other Baan customer had. Turned out to have performance issues (not to mention support issues). They subsequently migrated to Oracle (level 2 driver) in just a few months after going live.
